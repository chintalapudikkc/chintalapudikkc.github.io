A Brief History of Me

I am a principal researcher in the Networking Research Group at Microsoft Research Redmond (MSR). Prior to joining MSR Redmond, I have worked at Microsoft Research India, and Bosch Research and Technology Center in Palo Alto, CA, USA. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a PhD in Computer Science in 2006. My advisor was Prof. Ramesh Govindan. Prior to my PhD I obtained my Masters in Electrical Engineering (Signal Processing and Control Systems) from Drexel University in 2000 and B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from IIT Varanasi.

What I do

I have 18+ years of corporate research experience in conceiving inventing novel and disruptive research ideas and taking them to products and platform solutions. I am currently the architect of the next generation communication protocols between XBox console and accessories. In the past, I have pitched disruptive novel ideas and opportunities for technological innovation for novel products/services and led incubation efforts with external as well as internal customers to deliver them as solutions, some of which have now been incorporated into MS products such as Azure ML, Windows Phone etc.
